[this](https://github.com/Lemswell/portfolio-website/commit/0c9ee0dbcd5e3c650bf005d7d0f9af2e5d4eb79b) was the end of me.

I won't lie, I got lazy. I kept getting an interesting [react hydration error](https://nextjs.org/docs/messages/react-hydration-error) whose effect seemed negligible, making me think the fix would be easy... surely. Ah, but that is almost never the case with these types of bugs. 

Because of this preconception, I allowed myself to copy paste a solution for taking away scrollbars (for aesthetic purposes). But having copy pasted the solution resulted in my website looking like __ABSOLUTE DOOKIE__. I ended up committing the change because the _dookieness_ of the new aesthetic wouldn't have shown itself unless I re-ran my `npm run dev`- thingo (I am not knowledgeable enough to use technical terms, I was gonna say "re-rendered" but i'm not sure if thats the right term).

And so the _dookie_ slips past me and unto my website. Just like how it was when I didn't consider that people actually use light mode...

I told someone about my website, so we looked at it on their device, and it was in light mode... it looked so bad. 😭

And looking at it on you're phone didn't look too good either 🥲, so I finally made some changes that I think make me proud.
